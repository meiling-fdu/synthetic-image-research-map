# Luisa Verdoliva institution canonicalization audit

Audit date: 2026-07-03

## Scope and result

The audit covered the requested curated CSVs, both public preview JSON files,
and related manual/review/report artifacts. The refreshed preview contains 16
unique Luisa Verdoliva paper titles (17 paper-version records and 32 map
markers).

Ten unique titles had a stale `Federico II University Hospital` value before
this correction. Nine required new paper-level author–institution mappings.
The incremental-learning paper already had a correct curated mapping, but a
second OpenAlex paper version retained a stale `aggregated_institutions` value.

After refresh, no Luisa Verdoliva record contains any of these names in
`institution`, `current_institution`, `affiliations`,
`author_institution_affiliations`, or `aggregated_institutions`:

- `Federico II University Hospital`
- `University Hospital Federico II`
- `Federico II Hospital`

## Evidence and canonicalization decisions

- The local OpenAlex authorship rows for eight newly mapped papers preserve the
  raw affiliation `University Federico II of Naples` (with minor punctuation
  and address variants), while the resolved institution had drifted to the
  hospital entity.
- The paper PDF for *Are GAN Generated Images Easy to Detect?* names
  `University Federico II of Naples` for the listed author group.
- The ECCV PDF for *Zero-Shot Detection of AI-Generated Images* assigns Luisa
  Verdoliva to both Naples and Technical University of Munich. Both valid
  affiliations are preserved; the Naples institution is canonicalized and the
  TUM author indices remain intact.
- `University of Naples Federico II` already had a confirmed curated location
  in Naples. No hospital alias was added because a global alias could merge
  legitimate medical records incorrectly.
- A confirmed TUM Garching location was added so the valid second Zero-Shot
  marker remains exportable.

## Residual conflicts

There are no unresolved Luisa Verdoliva hospital/name conflicts in the curated
or public preview layers. Historical generated manual risk reports may still
describe the pre-fix snapshot; they were audited but not rewritten because
automated work must not overwrite files under `data/manual/`.
