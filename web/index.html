    About, methodology, and scope How records are selected, mapped, and explored

    Project scope

    The map covers detection of AI-generated, synthetic, GAN-generated, diffusion-generated, fake, and deepfake images, plus source attribution, identification, and verification for generated images.

    It excludes broad model, feature, authorship, camera-model, and sensor attribution, as well as work that only uses synthetic data for downstream tasks.

    Data and records

    The public preview combines automatically collected candidate metadata with eligible maintainer-confirmed records. It is a research-discovery aid, not a complete or uniformly curated bibliography.

    The map is institution-record based, so one paper may appear at several institutions. Affiliations are preserved per author and are not inferred from the first author. Unique papers view deduplicates records for bibliography-style browsing.

    data/manual/key_papers.csv is a coverage checklist for auditing only. Adding a paper there does not publish it to the map; not_covered_by_pipeline means the automatic pipeline did not cover that paper.

    Explore and export

    Keyword search, year range, task filters, the results-view toggle, and CSV export operate on the currently loaded dataset. Map markers and summary statistics remain institution based.

    Quality control

    Paper coverage and map coverage are different: a paper can be searchable while its affiliation or location still needs review. Map markers require a supported institution relationship and usable coordinates; uncertain, out-of-scope, low-confidence, and review-flagged marker records are excluded.
